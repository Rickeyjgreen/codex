Investigation queue

1. Build the clinical revenue bridge

For 2700, 4150, and 2100: census, occupied bed days, billable services, auth status, claims submitted, cash collected, denials, and timing lag.

2. Trace Liberty’s negative grant line

Pull the journal-entry history behind the -$138,592 Grants from Foundations YTD line in Liberty 2100 and determine whether this was a reversal, reclass, correction, or wrong-cost-center posting.

3. Reconcile billing, admin, and maintenance allocations

Validate methodology by cost center, especially 2700 vs 4150/4300 and 2100 vs 2165/5200.

4. Reconcile Women’s 4150 AD vs IS mismatch

Confirm the source of the approximately $2.7k current-month difference before using that center for precise accountability work.

5. Review active vs legacy cost centers

Decide whether 4300, 5200, and 2165 should remain active, be closed, or have expenses reassigned.

6. Audit Liberty facility-cost spikes

Utilities, janitorial, repairs, telecom, and security all need verification for catch-up billing, one-time work, or vendor creep.

7. Audit Women’s overages against operating model

Focus on billing allocation, client medical supplies, janitorial/grounds, and 4150 salary structure versus real staffing and usage.
